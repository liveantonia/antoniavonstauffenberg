Hi, I'm Antonia (most people call me Annie)

I am a 2nd year PhD student working on all things related to exoplanet atmospheric modelling. I am part of the AMouR (Atmospheric Modelling and Retrieval) group of Paul Mollière within the APEx Department at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy.

Within my PhD, I specialise mostly in the improvement of atmospheric retrievals with a main focus on clouds. I also have an interest in directly imaged planets and brown dwarfs, looking at variability and isotopologues. You can find my recent work on β Pictoris b under my publications.

When I'm not working, you will mainly find me horse riding, bouldering or reading.

Modelling Exoplanet Atmospheres

In the era of improved data quality with JWST, VLTI/GRAVITY+, and more, we have decidedly had to move away from simple 1-dimensional models and have understood that planets and their spectra are affected by their 3-dimensional nature. The best laboratory to study these effects is gas giants, from Hot Jupiters over young directly imaged planets and Brown Dwarfs. I focus on exploring their atmospheres by improving simple retrieval cloud models using petitRADTRANS (Mollière et al. 2019, Blain et al. 2024, Nasedkin et al. 2024) to bridge the gap to microphysical models without compromising on their flexibility and speed. As well as this, I examine the 3D temporal effects of clouds by studying their variability.
